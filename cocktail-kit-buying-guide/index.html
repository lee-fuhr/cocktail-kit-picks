How to choose a cocktail kit

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

A cocktail kit is a curated set of essential bar tools that helps you make drinks at home with confidence. The best kit for you depends on your skill level, budget, and how often you entertain, a quality set with stainless steel tools and a sturdy case will outlast cheaper alternatives and improve your mixing experience.

What should a good cocktail kit include?

A solid cocktail kit covers the basics you need to make most classic cocktails: a shaker (Boston or cobbler style), a jigger for measuring, a Hawthorne strainer, and a bar spoon for stirring and layering. Many sets also add a muddler for herbs and fruit, a citrus juicer, and a small cutting board or knife. Beyond the core tools, some kits throw in extras like bottle openers, ice tongs, or a recipe booklet. That can be helpful for beginners, but don’t let flashy extras distract you from the quality of the primary pieces. A well-made shaker and a reliable jigger matter far more than having a half-dozen accessories you rarely use.

What materials offer the best value and durability?

Stainless steel is the gold standard for cocktail tools. It resists rust, doesn’t react with acidic ingredients, and is easy to clean. Look for shakers and strainers made from 18/8 or 304 stainless steel, these hold up well even with daily use. Chromed brass or copper tools look beautiful but require careful drying and can chip or tarnish over time. Plastic or silicone utensils are budget-friendly and won’t break if dropped, but they lack the weight and precision of metal tools. If you’re investing in a kit you want to last, prioritize stainless steel over flashy finishes.

Should you buy a beginner kit or build your own?

Pre-assembled kits are convenient and often cost less than buying tools individually. They’re great for beginners who want one box with everything they need. The trade-off is that some kits include mediocre versions of each tool, you might end up replacing parts later. Building your own set lets you pick professional-grade pieces one at a time, but it takes more research and money upfront. If you’re just starting out, a well-reviewed kit from a reputable brand saves hassle. If you already know you’re serious about cocktails, start with a quality shaker and jigger, then add tools as you go.

What to look for in a cocktail shaker set?

The two main shaker styles are Boston shakers (two tins that seal together) and cobbler shakers (a built-in strainer and cap). Boston shakers are favored by professionals because they seal well, hold more liquid, and are easier to clean. Cobbler shakers are simpler to use for beginners but can get stuck and have a smaller capacity. Weighted tins are a key upgrade, they have a thicker rim that creates a tight seal and feel balanced in your hand. Cheaper unweighted tins can leak or dent. Whichever style you choose, look for shakers that fit snugly without rattling and have a smooth interior finish.

Is a portable or travel-friendly kit worth considering?

Compact cocktail kits designed for travel are handy if you often take drinks to parties, camping, or on vacation. They usually come in a small case with a mini shaker, a tiny jigger, and folding or collapsible tools. The downside is that mini tools sacrifice usability and accuracy. A small jigger can be hard to pour from, and a tiny strainer may clog. For occasional use they’re fine, but if you’re serious about cocktail-making at home, a full-sized kit will be much more satisfying to work with.

How much should you spend on a cocktail kit?

Cocktail kits range from very budget-friendly to several hundred dollars. At the entry level you get decent stainless steel tools with basic construction. In the mid-range you’ll find thicker metal, weighted shakers, and precision jiggers. Premium kits often include forged tools, custom cases, and better engineering. There’s no need to spend top dollar right away. A mid-range kit from a trusted barware brand will serve most home mixologists for years. Focus on the shaker and jigger quality, those are the tools you use every time. You can always upgrade a muddler or strainer later without buying a whole new set.

Frequently asked questions

Do I really need a muddler and a Hawthorne strainer?

A muddler is essential for drinks like mojitos and old fashioneds, but if you never make muddled cocktails you can skip it. A Hawthorne strainer is the standard for shaking, it fits the top of a Boston shaker tin and has a spring that filters out ice chips. You’ll use it almost every time you shake a drink.

Are weighted tins worth the extra cost?

Yes, for anyone who shakes frequently. Weighted tins create a better seal, feel more balanced, and are less likely to dent or get stuck. They cost a bit more but noticeably improve the shaking experience and the quality of your drinks.

Can I use a cocktail kit for non-alcoholic drinks?

Absolutely. A cocktail kit works great for making mocktails, fancy sodas, or shaken lemonades. The shaker, jigger, and strainer all serve the same purpose whether or not you’re using alcohol. Many of the same techniques, shaking, stirring, muddling, apply to alcohol-free recipes.

How do I clean and store my tools to prevent rust?

Hand wash stainless steel tools with warm soapy water immediately after use, then dry thoroughly with a soft cloth. Never put them in the dishwasher. Store them in a dry place, the case that came with your kit is fine, but avoid leaving wet tools inside a closed case where moisture can’t evaporate.

What’s the difference between a Boston shaker and a cobbler shaker?

A Boston shaker has two tins (one larger, one smaller) that you fit together, requiring a separate strainer. A cobbler shaker is a single piece with a built-in strainer and a cap. Boston shakers seal more reliably, hold more volume, and are easier to clean. Cobbler shakers are simpler to use but can be harder to open and have less capacity.

Should I choose a kit with a wooden or metal stand?

Wooden stands look classic and add a warm feel to your bar, but they can warp or stain if liquids soak in. Metal stands are more durable and easier to wipe down. Neither affects the quality of your cocktails, so pick based on your preference for looks and maintenance. Many good kits come with a sturdy case instead of a stand.

In shortA thoughtful cocktail kit can turn a random collection of tools into a daily pleasure. Focus on the pieces you’ll use every time, a solid shaker, a clear jigger, and a reliable strainer, and don’t be tempted by gimmicks. Whether you buy a complete set or build one piece by piece, good tools make better drinks and more enjoyable evenings behind the bar.
